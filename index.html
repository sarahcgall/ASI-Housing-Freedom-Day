2. Calendar Chart

Status: COMPLETE
The calendar chart (heatmap) is a Cost of Rent Freedom Day calculator designed to provide users with a visual of how much of their money goes to paying rent each year, based on their annual income and monthly rental costs. The chart also provides a comparator of various regions and sub-districts across England.

Title/Key Message:
How do you compare? Calculate your Cost of Rent Day

Features:
Region Selection: a dropdown option has been included for users to select different regions of England to compare themselves against. Each selected region appears on the graph to display the freedom day (in blue) and the days that they pay their landlords (in red). The region freedom day is based on area's mean or median income and average rental costs as per the ONS data. The dropdown menu is searchable for user-ease in finding their region or county amongst the 319 different options.

Freedom Day Calculator: users are able to calculate their own freedom day based on their income and rent using: freedomDay = 1 + ((12 * rent) / income) * 365.25. Once they select the "Calculate my Freedom Day" button, this will briefly change to "Calculating..." and then the calculated freedom day will appear on the chart. The delay in calculating is primarily to prevent users from repeatedly pressing the button which will trigger the function multiple times in a short period.

Error Handling: the chart throws an error in two circumstances: 1) if the income or rent have not been inputted; and 2) if the freedom day calculated goes beyond 2024. These error messages appear in a modal (pop up box) defined to the chart space.

Tooltip: a tooltip has been added to the calculated/personal freedom day. It provides the user with their personalised freedom date and details the number of days that they worked to pay their rental bill in 2024. Additionally, a tooltip has been added to the regional freedom day which also contains the income and rental information.

Other Interactivity: the individual days within the heatmap change opacity when the user hovers over them, creating a more engaging experience for users. There is also a short transitional delay has been added for the chart data to appear gradually.

Aesthetics: colours used are the ASI's branding colours. These have been tested and are colour blind safe.

Responsiveness: the chart scales across all devices, ensuring a consistent text size and preventing tick labels from overlapping.

3. Supply/Demand

Status: COMPLETE
The supply/demand chart (line chart) compares the number of permanent dwellings built in England vs. population estimates between 1971 and 2020. It helps highlight that new demand has outstripped supply.

Title/Key Message:
Since the 70s, England's construction of new homes has lagged behind population growth, signifying that new demand has outstripped supply

Features:
Responsiveness: the chart scales across all devices, ensuring a consistent text size and preventing tick labels from overlapping. To save space on smaller screens, the year changes from being "1971" format to "'70" format.

Aesthetics: colours have been used strategically to ensure user understanding of the dual y-axis, which can cause confusion. The ASI yellow has been chosen for the building houses colour against a contrasting black colour for population estimates. The area between the two paths is shaded in a grey diagonal pattern.

Tooltip: a tooltip has been added to show the year, number of dwellings, and population for additional information. The tooltip is not specific to placement and appears anywhere along the x-axis. For ease of comparison, a vertical line appears, connecting the two timeseries paths.

5. Timeseries

Status: CULLED
The timeseries chart (dumbbell chart) shows the percentage change in income and rent by region between 2019 and 2023. It indicates that there is some good news in most regions, with wage growth outpacing rental increases, thus easing the burden on renters.

Title/Key Message:
Over the past 5 years, wage growth has outpaced rental increases in most regions across England, shortening the Cost of Rent Day for renters

Features:
Responsiveness: the chart scales across all devices, ensuring a consistent text size and preventing tick labels from overlapping. The labels for the "Change in x" change to "Δ in x" in smaller screen sizes to avoid overlapping text. A descriptor of what the symbol means appears at this point under the y-axis labels.

Aesthetics: the ASI red has been chosen for the change in rent, ASI green for the change in income, and ASI blue for the change in Cost of Rent Day. These are to ensure consistency of categorising these variables amongst all charts.

Tooltip: a tooltip is yet to be added.

6. Histogram

Status: COMPLETE The purpose of the histogram is to show the frequencies of when each county's Cost of Rent Day falls within the year. This visual demonstrates the stark difference between London and the rest of England.

Title/Key Message:
While England marks its Cost of Rent Day, most local areas are still working solely to pay their annual rental bill

Features:
Responsiveness: the chart scales across all devices, ensuring a consistent text size and preventing tick labels from overlapping.

Aesthetics: the ASI blue for the individual Cost of Rent Day's has been chosen to ensure consistency of categories amongst all charts. For ease, and limiting the number of colours used, the highlight region option retains full opacity for the highlighted region while making the remaining regions have an opacity of 0.2.

Tooltip: a tooltip has been added to show the name of the local area. If the user uses the "Highlight Region" dropdown, the tooltip will be restricted to being visible for the local areas within the selected region only.

Other Interactivity: the user is able to highlight different regions so that this can be compared to the 5th of May (England's Cost of Rent Day). Limiting highlighting to just one region at a time aids the user's ability to digest the information and compare more effectively.
